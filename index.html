Hello, cyber surfer—

When we were growing up, the internet was all fun and games. But things have gotten pretty weird around here. Memes are being used as weapons in an information war. Outrage cycles loop round and round on a nauseating, neverending rollercoaster. People are getting sucked into radical rabbit holes.

What if we had medics on the battlefield of the online culture wars? Meme memedics. We're calling them Memedics.

This web site is where we're hashing out ideas about how Memedics should engage IRL and URL. We're working in public, trying to blur the line between published/unpublished, collecting research and music and art and good ideas about how to move towards memetic peace.



Principles we're playing with

  1. Memedics patiently and soberly investigate how people landed in their current belief system.
  2. Memedics acknowledge the (sometimes small) kernels of truth inside every point of view.
  3. Memedics work to understand people's internal motivations instead of dismissing them off-hand.
  4. Memedics understand that good people can get infected by bad ideas.
  5. Memedics know that outrage-fueled actions tend to provoke equal and opposite reactions.
  6. Memedics transcend toxic feedback loops by truly listening to people and meeting them where they're at.
  7. Memedics remember the self-fulfilling prophecy that undergirds the culture war: if you don't think people can evolve, they won't.
  8. Memedics practice curious humility, and they're not too concerned about being right.
  9. Memedics hope that they themselves will be transformed by their interactions with others.
